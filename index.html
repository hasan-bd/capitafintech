  • Essentials of Credit Scoring with AI.

    AI-based credit scoring is perhaps the most promising and relevant one. In a nutshell, credit scoring represents an evaluation of how well the bank's customer can pay and is willing to pay off debt. With 2.5 billion unbanked people globally and only less than one half of the banked population regarded as eligible for lending, the need for smarter credit scoring solutions is evident.

    AI credit scoring decisions are based on a lot of data, such as total income, credit history, transaction analysis, work experience, and even Google Analytics. In essence, scoring represents a mathematical model based on statistical methods and accounting for a large amount of information. As a result, credit scoring using AI provides more sensitive, individualized credit score assessments based on an array of additional real-time factors, giving access to finance to more people with income potential.

  • How Can AI Enhance Credit Risk Assessment?

    In most financial institutions, credit scoring models are still based on the scorecard approach, i.e., the dynamics characteristic for the time of their inception. A potential borrower has to possess sufficient historical data on previous borrowing behavior to be rated as "scorable." In case such historical information is absent (which is a typical situation for new customers of the banking sector), even creditworthy borrowers are denied access to credit. Unlike traditional credit scoring methods (e.g., the scorecard method) focusing on the past performance of the borrower, AI credit scoring is more sensitive to real-time indicators of the potential borrower's creditworthiness, such as the current level of income, employment opportunities, and their potential ability to earn. Thus, borrowers with high potential are included in the credit programs, while those who formally pass the conventional credit scoring assessment (e.g., credit card churners) are excluded from them. In other words, AI-based credit scoring allows precise profit predictions based on the smart AI models.

  • Advances of AI In Credit Scoring.

    Issuance of loans is always associated with risks, such as the recipient's failure to pay the loan back. Hence, the purpose of credit scoring is to minimize risk exposures, which caused a systemic problem of low access to credit finance. AI solutions transform credit scoring in many ways, signaling a paradigm shift in this aspect. Here are some evident bonuses of integrating AI into credit scoring.

  • Greater Customer Focus.

    Traditional credit scoring methods are repeatedly criticized as outdated, to a larger degree because of their standardization and lack of sensitivity to individual disparities and nuances. With AI introduction in credit scoring systems, banks get unique insights into their customers' financial behavior not only based on historical data, but also based on the potential income forecasting. Such analyses of large-scale customer data enable greater customer segmentation and rating in terms of associated credit risk, letting financial institutions price and sell their credit products to the right categories of clients.

  • Greater Speed.

    Specifically in credit scoring, AI applications are getting increasingly popular due to their ability to speed up the process of making lending decisions without any quality or precision compromises. Traditionally, banks applied decision trees, regression, and complicated arithmetical analyses to generate the client's credit score. Today, masses of superfluous, unstructured and partially structured data are included into analysis (e.g., social media use, mobile phone activities, etc.) to make smarter credit-related decisions, but with the help of AI, the speed of data processing remains high.

  • Greater Access to Credit.

    With the help of data science, credit scoring has become more future-oriented in contrast to old-school past-oriented approaches. In this way, more borrowers get access to credit today (e.g., students, founders of promising businesses, foreign residents), which stimulates their business and helps them jumpstart their ideas. Getting one's first-ever credit has also become simpler, which is now done based on the AI financial projections regarding the client's income potential and employment opportunities.

  • Benefits for All Stakeholders.

    The use of AI tools for credit scoring and lending decisions can not only increase the number of customers for the bank while decreasing the risk but also helps banks make data-driven decisions, focus on margin maximization instead of risk minimization, analyze smooth risk vs. profit curve instead of relying on pre-calculated scoring cards brackets. This is an approach that was virtually impossible before widespread implementation of AI and data gathering techniques. Both the banks and customers can benefit from adoption of AI in credit scoring: banks increase the number of customers and their profits, while the customers in need of credits get wider access to better lending products.
